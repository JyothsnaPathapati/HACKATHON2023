Singular: "cat," "book," "friend" Plural: "cats," "books," "friends" Verbs: Verbs are action words that describe what someone or something is doing. "She runs every morning." "They study for exams." Adjectives: Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns. "The red car is fast." "She has a friendly dog." Adverbs: Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs and answer questions like how, when, where, or to what degree. "He runs quickly." "She sings beautifully." Articles: Articles (a, an, the) are used before nouns to specify whether the noun is specific or nonspecific. "I want an apple." "She is reading the book." Pronouns: Pronouns are used to replace nouns to avoid repetition. "He is my brother. He is very nice." Prepositions: Prepositions show the relationship between a noun and other words in a sentence. "The book is on the table." "She's sitting in the car." Conjunctions: Conjunctions are used to connect words, phrases, or clauses. "I like both pizza and pasta." "She wanted to go, but he stayed home." Tenses: English verbs can be in different tenses to indicate when an action took place. Present Tense: "She plays the guitar." Past Tense: "She played the guitar." Future Tense: "She will play the guitar." Subject-Verb Agreement: Verbs must agree with the subject in terms of number and person. "She works in the city." "They work in the city." Direct and Indirect Objects: A direct object is the noun or pronoun that receives the action of the verb, while an indirect object is the recipient of the direct object. "She gave him a gift." (Him is the indirect object; a gift is the direct object.) Sentence Structure: A basic English sentence structure is subject-verb-object (SVO). "She (subject) eats (verb) pizza (object)." These are some fundamental English grammar rules. Practicing and understanding these rules can help improve your English language skills.
